Our Heritage

A Legacy Steeped in Flavor & Secret Whispers.

When White Oaks Restaurant in Westlake opened its doors in 1928, it was more than just an eatery – it was a clandestine speakeasy, a haven during an era of hidden pleasures. A night watchman’s quarters kept a vigilant eye out for police, ensuring patrons could discreetly enjoy alcohol imported from the finest distilleries in France, Canada, and Scotland.

Our walls, adorned with a captivating photo display, tell the rich story of this enduring establishment, originally brought to life by Mike Spooner. In 1971, Mike’s son, Byron, passed the torch to Anthony Small, whose son, Michael Small, proudly carries on the tradition today as the proprietor of White Oaks Restaurant. For nearly a century, we've remained a beacon of culinary excellence in Westlake.

This fine-dining spot is known for its timeless ambiance, impeccable service, and a commitment to quality that transcends generations. We blend classic techniques with fresh, local ingredients, creating an unforgettable dining experience that honors our past while celebrating the present.
